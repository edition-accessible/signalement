Descriptive sentences

Moby Dick; Or, The Whale.

Herman Melville

The sailor Ishmael's narrative of the maniacal quest of Ahab, captain of the whaling ship Pequod, for vengeance against Moby Dick, the giant white whale that bit off his leg on the ship's previous voyage.

Product details

  • EPUB Protected by LCP
  • Published in 1851 by Richard Bentley (United Kingdom)
  • ISBN 9780000000000

Accessibility

All content available for computer-generated speech and braille reading.

Appearance of the text and page layout can be modified according to the capabilities of the reading system (font family and font size, spaces between paragraphs, sentences, word, and letter, as well as color of background and texts).

Navigation:

  • Contains a table of contents that provides direct access to all chapters of the text via links.
  • Index provides links to item references.
  • Landmarks provides quick access to main parts of the book.
  • A page list enables users to coordinate their reading with a statically paginated version.

No hazards.

This publication is certified to meet accepted accessibility standards.

Additional Accessibility Information
  • This publication meets accepted accessibility recommendations and reports (EPUB Accessibility 1.1 and Web Accessibility Content Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA.)
  • This publication was certified on 2021-09-07.
  • This publication is certified by Foo's Accessibility Testing (a third-party).
  • The certifier's credential is "Enterprise Accessibility Rating."
  • For more information, refer to the certifier's report.

La Marque des quatre

Arthur Conan Doyle

La Marque des quatre ou Le Signe des quatre (The Sign of Four en version originale) est la deuxième aventure de Sherlock Holmes, écrite en 1889 par Arthur Conan Doyle et publiée en 1890. Ennuyé par le manque d'enquêtes, Sherlock Holmes s'adonne à la consommation de drogues pour s'occuper, ainsi qu'à diverses activités ennuyeuses, sous le regard de son colocataire, le docteur John Watson.
